I think we all have some kind of appreciation for

nature, but I have a lot of it. Mountains, lakes, rivers,

glaciers, volcanoes, boreal forests, icebergs, waterfalls,

insane rock formations, epic sunsets, dogs, say whaa..

dogs? Yes I freakin' love dogs.

Dawg have you ever woken up to the fresh crisp

mountain air? Or to the sight of the first light of dawn

hitting the slope of the mountain? Imagine waking up

and knowing there's a promise of new adventures. And

not to mention the campfire after a day of sending it.

It's winter: nights are long, temperatures are frigid.

Time to head to the subarctic to scout the dark skies

for the northern lights and the milky way? Sounds like

a stellar idea to me. Hey, it's crazy that you can't feel

lonely under a clear night sky.

Photography is one of the many ways I celebrate

nature, I love sharing my stories and adventures

through it and filmmaking. Shoot me an email if you

wanna go out and take some bangers!

Aight real talk. I'm very fortunate to be able to realize

my dream of becoming a pilot. Whenever I go flying, I

never take it for granted, but rather reminded of how

privileged I am. Flying would be one of the things that

can make me look back at life one day and say "I sure

did have fun".
